Causes of climate change

Here you can find information on the Causes of climate change.







Burning Fossil Fuels

Burning fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and gas for cars, factories, and electricity releases large amounts of carbon dioxide into the air. Carbon dioxide is a greenhouse gas that traps heat in the atmosphere and makes the Earth warmer. The more fossil fuels we burn, the faster global warming increases.

Deforestation

Deforestation means cutting down large numbers of trees and forests. Trees normally absorb carbon dioxide, so when they are removed, more carbon stays in the atmosphere. This not only increases global warming but also destroys habitats for animals and reduces biodiversity.

Plastic Waste

Plastic is made from fossil fuels and takes hundreds of years to break down. When plastic is burned or left in landfills, it releases harmful gases and chemicals into the environment. Plastic pollution also damages oceans, harms wildlife, and contributes indirectly to climate change.

Energy Overuse

Using too much electricity, especially from non-renewable sources, increases greenhouse gas emissions. Leaving lights on, wasting water, and overusing air conditioning all add extra pressure on power plants. Saving energy helps reduce pollution and slows climate change.





Effects of climate change

Here you can find information on the Effects of climate change.







Rising Temperatures

The Earth’s average temperature is increasing every year. Hotter temperatures lead to heatwaves, dry land, and melting ice caps. This makes some places uncomfortable or even unsafe to live in.

Extreme Weather

Climate change causes stronger and more frequent natural disasters such as floods, hurricanes, droughts, and wildfires. These events destroy homes, farms, and cities, and can put many lives in danger.

Melting Ice & Rising Sea Levels

Glaciers and polar ice are melting because of higher temperatures. This causes sea levels to rise, which can flood coastal areas and small islands. Many communities near oceans are at risk

Harm to Animals and Plants

Many animals lose their natural habitats due to changing temperatures and weather patterns. Some species cannot adapt fast enough and may become endangered or extinct. Ecosystems become unbalanced.

Impact on Humans

Climate change affects food supply, clean water, and human health. People may suffer from heat illnesses, hunger, and lack of safe drinking water, especially in hotter regions of the world.
